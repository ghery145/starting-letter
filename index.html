Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! as part of my post-doctorate work, i did study exotic tribal dance. To answer your question. my favourite dances are as follows, with defenitions:

Polynesian chicken dance

A little know but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock or be "fruitful".

icelandic brownian shuffle

Before the icelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which involved buddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptably tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired modern styles such as twerking.

Artic Robot Dance

an intersting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s belived to have discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic",stilted movements, being praticed by inhabitans of Northern Alaska and canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold.
